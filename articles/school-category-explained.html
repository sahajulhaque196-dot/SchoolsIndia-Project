Primary, Upper Primary, Secondary, Higher Secondary: India's School Categories Explained

By Sahajul  ·  Tezpur, Assam  ·  January 2025  ·  10 min read

One of the most common questions I receive from parents using Schoolspedia.in is about school categories. What exactly is the difference between a 'Primary' school and an 'Upper Primary' school? What does 'Secondary with Higher Secondary' mean? And what does a school's category tell you about what grades your child can study there? These are entirely reasonable questions — the terminology is not self-explanatory, and it matters for parents making decisions about their children's schooling.

Primary Schools

A Primary school in India's education classification system covers Classes 1 through 5, catering to children roughly aged 6 to 11. These are the foundational years of formal education where children learn basic literacy, numeracy, and elementary concepts across subjects. Primary schools are the most numerous type of school in the UDISE database — there are more of them than any other category, particularly in rural India, because the government policy has been to ensure that every child has a primary school within walking distance of their home.

Upper Primary Schools

Upper Primary schools cover Classes 6 through 8, catering to children roughly aged 11 to 14. Many schools that are listed as 'Primary with Upper Primary' in the UDISE database offer both levels — Classes 1 through 8 — under one roof. The transition from primary to upper primary is an important one academically: the curriculum becomes more subject-specific and demands a higher level of literacy and numeracy. This transition is also a significant dropout point, particularly for girls in rural areas.

Secondary Schools

Secondary schools cover Classes 9 and 10. At the end of Class 10, students typically sit for the state board or CBSE/ICSE examination that leads to the Secondary School Certificate (SSC or equivalent). The Class 10 board examination is, for many students, their first major formal examination and an important credential. Secondary school requires students to choose between different subject streams in some systems, and is generally more academically demanding than upper primary.

Higher Secondary Schools

Higher Secondary schools cover Classes 11 and 12. At this level, students typically choose a stream — Science, Commerce, or Arts/Humanities — and the curriculum becomes significantly more specialised. The Class 12 board examination results determine eligibility for undergraduate admissions across India. Higher Secondary schools are less numerous than primary schools, which means that in rural areas, students may have to travel significant distances to attend Classes 11 and 12.

Combined Category Schools

Many schools in India offer multiple levels under one roof, which is reflected in the combined category descriptions you see in the UDISE database. A school listed as 'Primary with Upper Primary, Secondary and Higher Secondary' (or abbreviated variations of this) is a school that offers classes from 1 through 12. These comprehensive schools are more common in urban areas and are highly valued by parents for the continuity and convenience they provide.
