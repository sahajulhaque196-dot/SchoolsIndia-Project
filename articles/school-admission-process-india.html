School Admission Process in India: A Complete Guide for First-Time Parents

By Sahajul  ·  Tezpur, Assam  ·  January 2025  ·  10 min read

Navigating the school admission process in India for the first time can feel overwhelming. The rules vary by state, by school type, by education board, and sometimes by individual school. I have helped several families in my community in Tezpur understand this process, and the same questions come up again and again. This guide tries to answer the most common ones.

Government School Admission

For government schools, admission is generally straightforward. Under the Right to Education Act, every child between 6 and 14 has the right to be admitted to the neighbourhood government school. You go to the school with proof of age (birth certificate or equivalent), proof of residence, and any other documents the school or local education office requires. Government schools cannot refuse admission to a child in this age group, cannot conduct interviews or tests for admission at the primary level, and cannot charge capitation fees.

Private School Admission

Private school admissions are more varied and often more competitive, particularly for sought-after schools in urban areas. Most private schools have their own admission processes, which may include application forms, entrance tests (for older age groups), and waiting lists. At the nursery and junior kindergarten level, the Supreme Court and various state governments have placed restrictions on the admission criteria private schools can use — they cannot conduct tests for very young children or base admissions on parental qualifications.

RTE 25 Percent Quota

If you want to use the 25 percent EWS quota at a private school under the RTE Act, the process typically involves registering with the state education department, providing proof of income and residence, and participating in a lottery if there are more applicants than available seats. The specific process varies by state, and some states have moved the application process online. The seats are genuinely free — the school is reimbursed by the government — but parents should be aware that being in a minority group in a fee-paying school can sometimes create social challenges for children.

Documents You Will Need

The documents typically required for school admission include: proof of age (birth certificate, hospital discharge certificate, or an affidavit if no formal certificate is available), proof of residence (ration card, electricity bill, Aadhaar card), passport-size photographs, and, for transfer admissions, a transfer certificate from the previous school. Government schools are generally required to accept children even if they cannot produce all documents, as the RTE Act prohibits refusing admission on documentation grounds.

What to Look for When Choosing

When choosing between schools, visit in person. Look at the physical condition of the classrooms and toilets. Observe whether children seem engaged and whether teachers are present and teaching. Ask other parents about their experience. Check the school's UDISE profile on Schoolspedia.in to understand its management type, category, and basic infrastructure. And ask the school directly about teacher turnover — high turnover is often a sign of underlying problems with school management.
