Types of School Management in India Explained: Government, Aided, Unaided and More

By Sahajul  ·  Tezpur, Assam  ·  January 2025  ·  10 min read

When I look at school pages on Schoolspedia.in, one of the fields that visitors sometimes find confusing is the 'management type'. A school might be listed as 'Department of Education', or 'Local Body', or 'Private Aided', or 'Private Unaided'. For parents trying to understand what kind of school their child attends, these distinctions matter — they affect fee structures, curriculum oversight, teacher employment conditions, and the level of government accountability.

Government Schools

Government schools managed by the state's Department of Education are the most common type of school in India, particularly in rural areas. These schools are funded entirely by the state government, teachers are government employees with civil service status and salaries, and education is provided free of charge (or at very nominal cost). Curriculum and examination standards are set by the respective State Board. Government schools are subject to government oversight and are required to participate in various central and state education schemes.

Local Body Schools

Local body schools are managed by municipal corporations, municipalities, gram panchayats, or other local government bodies rather than directly by the state education department. In practice, local body schools are very similar to government schools in terms of funding, fee structure, and teacher employment conditions. The key difference is the administrative chain — a local body school answers to the municipality or panchayat rather than to the district education office.

Private Aided Schools

Private aided schools are privately managed institutions that receive financial assistance — typically in the form of teacher salary grants — from the state government. In exchange for this aid, they are subject to government oversight on admissions, fees, and curriculum to varying degrees depending on state policy. Many private aided schools are run by trusts, religious organisations, or community bodies. They often have a longer history than newer private unaided schools, and in states like Kerala, they are deeply embedded in the education system.

Private Unaided Schools

Private unaided schools receive no government financial assistance and are funded entirely by fees paid by students' families. They set their own fee structures (subject to state-level fee regulation in some states) and employ teachers on their own terms. They may be affiliated to the CBSE, ICSE, or state board. Private unaided schools range from elite institutions charging lakhs per year to budget schools in semi-urban areas charging a few hundred rupees per month.

Central Government Schools

A small but important category includes schools run directly by central government bodies — Kendriya Vidyalayas (run by the Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan), Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalayas, and schools run by various central government departments for the children of their employees. These schools follow CBSE curriculum, charge very low fees, and are considered among the better-performing government schools in the country.
