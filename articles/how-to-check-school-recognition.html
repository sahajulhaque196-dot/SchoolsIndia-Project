Is Your Child's School Officially Recognised? How to Verify in 5 Minutes

By Sahajul  ·  Tezpur, Assam  ·  January 2025  ·  10 min read

I receive more questions about this than almost any other education topic. A family moves to a new area, their child starts attending a school that looks decent, and then someone mentions that the school might not be 'recognised' — and suddenly there is uncertainty and anxiety about whether the education their child is receiving will count toward future qualifications. This is a genuinely important question, and one that is more straightforward to answer than many people realise.

What Does School Recognition Mean?

In India, school recognition refers to the formal approval granted by a competent authority — typically the state government's education department — to operate as an educational institution. A recognised school has met certain minimum standards regarding infrastructure, staffing, and curriculum, and is entitled to present students for public examinations at the relevant level. An unrecognised school has not received this approval and cannot officially prepare students for board examinations.

Why It Matters

The practical consequences of attending an unrecognised school can be significant. Students from unrecognised schools cannot appear in state board or CBSE/ICSE examinations. Certificates and degrees obtained from unrecognised institutions are not accepted for government jobs, higher education admissions, or most formal employment. For a child who spends six or eight years in an unrecognised school, this can mean that their entire primary education is effectively wasted in terms of formal credentials.

How to Verify Using UDISE

The simplest way to check whether a school is officially recognised is to look it up in the UDISE database. A school that appears in UDISE has been registered with the government's education information system and has submitted data, which means it has some level of official standing. On Schoolspedia.in, you can search for any school by name, district, or UDISE code. If the school is in the database and shows a status of 'Operational', that is a good initial indicator.

Other Verification Methods

For a more definitive verification, you can contact the District Education Office (DEO) in the district where the school is located. The DEO maintains records of all recognised schools in the district. You can visit in person or, in many states, check the education department's website. Another option is to ask the school directly to show you their Recognition Certificate — a legitimately recognised school should be willing to produce this document. Be cautious of schools that are evasive or give complicated explanations about why they cannot immediately show you this document.
