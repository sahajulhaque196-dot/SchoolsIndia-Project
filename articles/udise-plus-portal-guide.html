How to Use the UDISE+ Portal: A Step-by-Step Guide for Parents and Researchers

By Sahajul  ·  Tezpur, Assam  ·  January 2025  ·  10 min read

The UDISE+ portal — available at udiseplus.gov.in — is the official government website for accessing school data in India. It contains comprehensive information about every school that has submitted data to the national education database, including details about infrastructure, enrolment, teachers, and mid-day meal implementation. For parents, researchers, journalists, and education administrators, it is an invaluable resource. It is also, frankly, not the most user-friendly website in the world. This guide will walk you through how to find what you need.

What Information Is Available on UDISE+

The UDISE+ portal provides several categories of information. For individual schools, you can find: basic identification data (name, UDISE code, address), infrastructure data (number of classrooms, toilet facilities, drinking water, electricity, library, computers), enrolment data by grade and gender, teacher data (number of teachers by qualification and type), and mid-day meal data. Some of this data is updated annually; other fields are updated less frequently.

Finding a Specific School

To find a specific school on UDISE+, go to udiseplus.gov.in and look for the school search function. You can search by UDISE code (if you have it), by school name, or by location (state, district, block). The search can be imprecise — schools may be listed under slightly different name spellings — so it is worth trying variations if your first search does not find the school. Once you locate the school, click on its name to access the full data page.

District and State Reports

For district and state level data, UDISE+ provides downloadable reports that aggregate school data at various geographic levels. These reports are particularly useful for researchers and policymakers. The portal also provides comparison tools that allow you to compare indicators across districts or states. Note that some of these comparison features work better in desktop browsers than on mobile.

Understanding the Data

A word of caution about interpreting UDISE data: the information is only as accurate as what schools have reported to the database. Self-reported data has inherent limitations. A school may report having a library when what exists is a cupboard with a few textbooks. Enrolment figures may not reflect actual attendance. Infrastructure that was present when data was last submitted may since have deteriorated. Use the data as a starting point for understanding a school's profile, not as a guarantee of current conditions.
