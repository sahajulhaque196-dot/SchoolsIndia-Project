About Schoolspedia.in

Last updated: January 2025  ·  Written by Sahajul, Tezpur, Assam

My name is Sahajul, and I live in Tezpur — a small but vibrant town nestled along the banks of the Brahmaputra in Assam. I have spent the better part of the last fourteen years writing about education, rural development, and the everyday realities of life in Northeast India. And it is from that lived experience — watching parents struggle to find reliable information about schools near their villages — that Schoolspedia.in was born.

I remember a conversation I had with a neighbour a few years ago. Her family had recently moved to a new block, and she was desperately trying to find out which government school near her new home offered classes up to Class 10. She asked three different people, got three different answers, and eventually had to make a half-day journey just to check for herself. That small frustration stayed with me. In a country with over sixteen lakh schools, why was it so hard to find basic, accurate information about them?

Why We Built This

Schoolspedia.in is my attempt to solve that problem — once and for all, and for everyone. This website is a free, comprehensive directory of every school in India that is listed under the UDISE (Unified District Information System for Education) database maintained by the Ministry of Education, Government of India. We have taken that official data and made it searchable, readable, and useful for ordinary people.

Whether you are a parent trying to find the nearest primary school in your block, a student researching schools for admission, a journalist covering the state of education in rural India, or a researcher who needs to verify school data — this website is for you. No sign-up. No paywall. No hidden charges. Just information, presented clearly.

What You Will Find Here

Every school listed on Schoolspedia.in has its own dedicated page. On each page, you will find the school's official name, UDISE code, full address (state, district, block, village, and cluster), management type (government, private aided, private unaided, local body, central government), school category (primary, upper primary, secondary, higher secondary), whether the school is co-educational or not, and the current operational status.

We have also added demographic context for each school's district — population figures, literacy rates, sex ratios, and other census data — so that you can understand the broader educational landscape of the area. This additional layer of information, drawn from official census records, makes Schoolspedia.in more than just a directory. It is a resource for understanding Indian education at the ground level.

About the Data

All school data on this website is sourced from the UDISE database published by the Ministry of Education, Government of India. UDISE is the most authoritative and comprehensive source of school-level education data in the country, covering over 16 lakh schools across all 36 states and union territories.

District-level demographic data is sourced from the Census of India. We have cross-referenced these datasets carefully to ensure accuracy. That said, data changes over time — schools open, close, merge, or relocate. We update our database periodically, but we always recommend verifying critical details directly with the school or the relevant District Education Office.

Who I Am

I have been writing about education and rural life in India for fourteen years. My work has taken me to government schools in remote tribal blocks of Assam, to one-teacher schools in the hills of Meghalaya, and to urban private schools in Guwahati and Kolkata. That range of experience has given me a deep respect for teachers working in difficult conditions, and a genuine belief that access to information is one of the most powerful tools we can give to families making decisions about their children's education.

I built Schoolspedia.in because I wanted to create something useful — not just content for search engines, but a real resource that a parent in a remote village could actually use. If this website has helped even one family make a more informed decision, it has served its purpose.

Contact and Corrections

If you find any incorrect information on this website, I genuinely want to know about it. Data errors are inevitable in a project of this scale, and correcting them is something I take seriously. You can reach me at schoolspedia@gmail.com. Please include the school name, UDISE code, and the specific information you believe is incorrect, along with any source you can point me to. I will review and update the page as quickly as possible.

Thank you for visiting Schoolspedia.in. I hope it is useful to you.
